 A Message from the Director 
From a Handful of Believers to a
Movement Across Two Continents
Rev. Dr. Sherwyn Ramey  ·  Director, Foreign Missions  ·  March 2026
"What began in 2021 has become a movement of faith in action — and your faithfulness is the reason lives are being transformed."

Dear CMBC Family, March is Foreign Mission Emphasis Month — a sacred season when we turn our hearts, prayers, and generosity toward the global work God has entrusted to us. In just five years, CMBC's foreign missions work has grown from two small fellowships into a movement spanning 27 churches and more than 2,500 members across India and Kenya.

Under the visionary leadership of our Senior Pastor, Rev. Oscar D. Epps, CMBC Foreign Missions continues to embody holistic ministry — evangelism, discipleship, humanitarian outreach, leadership training, and compassionate care. Sanctuaries have been built. Wells have been drilled. Children are being fed. Leaders are being raised up. And souls are being saved.

Read on for field reports, a transparent look at how your gifts are invested, and an invitation to be part of this extraordinary Kingdom movement. We are grateful for every prayer and every dollar.

Rev. Dr. Sherwyn Ramey
Director, Foreign Missions

 Mission Field Reports — Q1 2026 
India — Andhra Pradesh

Baptism ceremony at the newly constructed pool — Andhra Pradesh, India.

21 Churches. 2,000+ Members. A Thriving Movement.
Est. 2021  ·  Led by Pastor Kumar Paul
$1,000 Funded the
Baptism Pool

What began in a dilapidated structure has grown into 21 churches and 2,000+ members. The main sanctuary seats 250 with a baptism pool now constructed. Over 1,800 baptisms total — 212 baptized in Q1 2026 alone. Through the EPPS School of Ministry, 120+ ministers have been trained for sustained Kingdom growth.

Two bore wells provide clean water to 150+ villagers daily. Food distribution feeds 250+ children every day. A two-story facility is under construction for pastoral housing and street children. CMBC Cedar Hill has sent over 900 pairs of shoes and 500+ Bibles in local dialects.

  • 285 New members Q1 2026
  • 212 Baptisms Q1 2026
  • 2 Bore wells — 150+ served daily
  • 250+ Children fed every day
  • $19,800+ Invested to date
Kenya — Nyamira

Believers baptized in the river — Nyamira, Kenya.

6 Churches. 500+ Members. A Village Transformed.
Est. 2021  ·  Led by Bishop Dr. Mike Peter Mogi
$2,500 Water Well —
Village Served

From a shed with a handful of members, CMBC Kenya has grown to 6 churches and 500+ members. Q1 2026 saw 38 new members and 28 baptisms. The EPPS School of Ministry has trained 50+ ministers, with 15 ordained for indigenous leadership and church planting across the region.

A 600-seat multipurpose worship facility is actively under construction with classrooms, offices, and a full kitchen. A water well now serves the entire Nyamira village. The Eyes on Kenya Initiative delivered eyecare to 700+ individuals. Weekly food distribution reaches 500+ children every week.

  • 38 New members Q1 2026
  • 28 Baptisms Q1 2026
  • 15 Ministers ordained
  • 700+ Eyes on Kenya served
  • $29,850+ Invested to date

 Our Purpose for Missions 

Recognizing that the missionary mandate permeates all of Scripture, our purpose is grounded in five biblical commitments:

  1. Participate in God's Plan of Blessing for All Nations
    Every nation, tribe, and tongue is included in God's redemptive plan. We join His work through the Gospel.
    Genesis 3:15; 12:3 · Psalm 47:7–9 · Romans 15:8–12
  2. Bring the Saving Knowledge of Jesus to All People
    Every person deserves the opportunity to hear and respond to the Gospel, regardless of geography or culture.
    John 3:16–18 · Romans 10:13–15 · Isaiah 49:6
  3. Pursue True Worshipers from Every Tribe and Tongue
    We join God in gathering worshipers from every corner of the earth — united as one body in Christ.
    John 4:23 · Psalm 67:3–4
  4. Grow Christ's Church and Further God's Kingdom
    We partner globally to plant, strengthen, and equip local churches for Kingdom advance.
    Matthew 16:18; 24:14 · Ephesians 4:11–13
  5. Share Christ's Compassion — Body and Soul
    Meeting physical needs — food, water, healthcare — is inseparable from proclaiming the Gospel.
    Mark 9:41 · 2 Corinthians 8:1–4 · Matthew 25:34–36
Our Vision in Action

We accomplish our purpose by:

  • Integrating missions into our teaching
  • Balancing local and global giving
  • Sending equipped ministers to the nations
  • Humanitarian aid alongside the Gospel
  • Training leaders — EPPS School of Ministry

Foreign Mission Emphasis Month: How to Participate
🙏 Pray

Commit to daily prayer for our pastors, congregations, and communities in India and Kenya. Pray for new believers, growing leaders, and ongoing provision for construction and feeding programs.

🧡 Give

Special offerings during March support the 600-seat Kenya sanctuary, two-story India facility, feeding programs, and water wells. Every gift makes an eternal difference — give at communitymbc.org/give.

✈️ Go

Are you called to go? Consider joining an upcoming mission trip to India or Kenya. Contact our Foreign Missions office to learn how you can serve firsthand alongside our partners on the field.

What Is Foreign Mission Emphasis Month?
  • Awareness & Education — Learn about our mission partners in India and Kenya
  • Prayer Support — Pray specifically for our pastors, churches, and communities abroad
  • Financial Giving — Give toward ongoing construction, food distribution, and ministry needs
  • Connecting with Missionaries — Hear firsthand testimonies from the field
  • Mobilization — Consider how you are called to go, pray, or give
